#### 2c. High-Δ teams currently classified as Cohesive

**Key question:** the classifier is measuring distance to a hand-crafted "extreme" Conflict/Collusive archetype. Teams like Team 6 may have anomalous dynamics that are anomalous _in a different direction_ from our prototypes — not captured by the current label set.

### 3. Discussion Points

#### 3a. Are the synthesised archetypes too extreme?

Every team in the dataset falls closer to the Cohesive archetype than to Conflict or Collusive. The Mahalanobis distances to Conflict (≈58–60) are ~6× larger than to Cohesive (≈7–11), suggesting the Conflict prototype is far outside the convex hull of real data.

**Options:**
- (A) Accept that the dataset is genuinely cohesive-leaning (capstone teams self-select for cooperation) and the labels are meaningful even if sparse
- (B) Calibrate the synthesised archetypes to real data percentiles rather than absolute extremes
- (C) Let the AA-learned archetypes speak first — if k=2 is the most stable split, the data may only support a binary Typical / Anomalous taxonomy, with the 5-label system as a refinement for clearly extreme cases

#### 3b. AA stability tells a different story from the elbow

| k | RSS | Bootstrap stability |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | 1789 | **0.917** |
| 3 | 1565 | 0.682 |
| 4 | 1375 | 0.591 |
| 5 | 1203 | 0.500 |
| 6 | 1048 | 0.478 |
| 7 | 926 | 0.538 |
| 8 | 815 | 0.602 |

The elbow (maximum perpendicular RSS drop) selects k=5, but **k=2 is by far the most stable** (0.92). This may mean the dataset cleanly separates into two behavioural modes — "typical" and "outlier" — with the finer 5-archetype taxonomy being over-specified for this data.

**Discussion:** Should we report both k=2 and k=5 as findings? What does Anna think the right granularity is?

#### 3c. Multi-label gap: non-submitter teams are not captured

Teams with non-submitters are not labelled differently from fully-submitting cohesive teams. The non-submitter fraction is in the feature vector but the synthesised archetypes don't place it prominently. Team 6's high Δ may be driven purely by the non-submitter rather than a true "Conflict" dynamic.

**Proposal:** Add a separate "Non-submitter present" flag to the classification output, since this is a directly observable condition (not an inferred dynamic) and already exists in the data quality report.

#### 3d. Next step: integrate into the dashboard as pattern flags

Issue #11's acceptance criteria includes an instructor-facing dashboard where high-Δ teams are cross-referenced with observable score patterns. The `classifications.csv` output can now serve as that source:
- Teams with label ≠ Cohesive → pattern flag
- Teams with Δ > 75th-percentile threshold → red-flag
- Combining both: the most useful signal for instructors
