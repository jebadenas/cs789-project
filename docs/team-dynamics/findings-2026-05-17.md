# Findings & Decision Record — 2026-05-17

**Status:** method pivoted and implemented; results computed; data-quality limitation surfaced. Continue next session.

---

## TL;DR

The 5-prototype classification approach was abandoned. It collapsed 102/105
matrices into "Cohesive" because the hand-crafted prototypes sit far outside
the real data cloud, so nearest-prototype always returned the least-extreme
label. Evidence showed the data is **unimodal with a heavy tail**, not five
clusters. RQ3 was reframed from *classification* to a **continuous atypicality
score** (Mahalanobis distance from the data centroid), with a secondary binary
Typical/Anomalous flag from a chi-square cutoff.

Headline result (clean subset): atypical teams disagree across models ~10×
more than typical teams (mean Δ 2.12 vs 0.22). The continuous correlation is
modest (r=0.37, p=0.02, n=39). A major data-quality finding emerged: of 35
teams, only 18 are usable and only 6 are complete.

---

## Why the pivot (evidence)

| Evidence | Value | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| AA bootstrap stability k=2 | 0.92 | a binary split is robust |
| AA bootstrap stability k=5 | 0.50 | five groups are not reproducible (chance) |
| Prototype Mahalanobis distances | Cohesive ~9 … Conflict ~58 | prototypes lie outside the real data cloud |
| Feature distribution | one mode + long tail | not 5 clusters; classification is the wrong frame |

Recalibrating the five prototypes (a "data-derived extremes" option that was
considered) would fix the distance scale but not the underlying reality: the
data does not contain five groups, so any 5-way scheme imposes structure that
bootstrap resampling cannot recover.

## Decisions taken this session

1. **Unit of analysis:** per (team × question_label). Keeps n=105 rows;
   non-independence (a team contributes up to 3 correlated rows) must be
   reported as a front-line limitation, not a footnote.
2. **Degenerate definition:** non-submitter present OR zero rater variation
   ("flat"). Both produce a no-signal matrix.
3. **Degenerate handling:** flag and keep; report results on the full set and
   the clean subset so the confound is visible.
4. **Binary flag source:** chi-square cutoff on squared Mahalanobis distance
   (d² ~ χ²(df)); principled, no hand-picked threshold.

## Empirical findings

### Atypicality result (clean subset, n=39)

| Group | n | Mean Δ |
|---|---|---|
| Anomalous | 12 | 2.12 |
| Typical | 27 | 0.22 |

- Continuous correlation: clean r=+0.366, p=0.022, n=39.
- Full-set r=+0.515 (p≈2e-8) is **inflated** by the degenerate confound
  (non-submission drives both atypicality and Δ). The clean figure is the
  defensible one. The "report both" choice exposed this exactly as intended.
- The old method was blind precisely where it matters: Team 6 (Caffeine
  Overload), Δ=7.06, was labelled Cohesive with weight 0.98. The new method
  ranks it top-atypical and clean.
- Honest framing: atypicality is a strong **filter** (typical teams almost
  never disagree), not a tight linear predictor. The group contrast is the
  strong result; the linear correlation is modest.

### Data-quality finding (co-headline)

- **Total: 35 teams** (105 team-matrices), from only **2 course sessions**
  (COMPSCI399 S1 2023 Session 3 = 20 teams; S1 2024 Session 4 = 15 teams),
  3 questions per team. Nothing dropped in featurization.
- **66/105 matrices unusable**, split by cause: 28 flat, 24 non-submitter,
  14 both. Two distinct failure modes: engagement (flat) vs participation
  (non-submitter).
- Distinct teams: **6 fully usable** (all 3 questions clean), **12 partially
  usable**, **17 fully dead**. Working dataset = **39 matrices from 18
  teams**.
- Root cause is upstream: small teams + voluntary form + flat-clicking,
  compounded over 3 questions. The lever is data availability, not method.
